## What's Happening with the Web

For thirty years, we've built websites for human eyes. Now AI agents are visiting these sites, trying to understand content to answer user questions.

**The problem:** Most websites are invisible to AI. They can't parse unstructured content.

**The result:**

- Businesses don't appear in AI-assisted discovery
- AI makes up facts when it can't find clear data
- Users get unreliable information

**Example:** When someone asks an AI "What are the opening hours for Smith's Bakery?", the AI often guesses because the information isn't structured in a way it can reliably parse.

---

## What MX Does

MX (Machine Experience) adds structure to web content so AI can understand it:

| Human-Readable | Machine-Readable |
|---------------|-----------------|
| "Open Mon-Sat, 8am-6pm" | Schema.org OpeningHoursSpecification |
| "Â£99" | Explicit price with currency |
| "Call us" | ContactPoint with defined method |

**The key insight:** The same patterns that help AI also help people with disabilities (screen readers, keyboard navigation). It's a convergence - improving for one audience improves for both.

---

## Why This Matters Now

In January 2026, Amazon, Microsoft, and Google all launched AI shopping assistants within seven days of each other. AI-mediated interaction is becoming infrastructure.

Websites that AI can understand will be discovered and recommended. Websites that AI can't understand will be invisible.
