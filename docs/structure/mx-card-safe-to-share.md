# MX-Card System

## Giving Physical Objects a Voice for AI Agents

**A Public Overview**

---

## The Problem

AI agents are becoming our everyday assistants. They book appointments, research purchases, plan travel, and help us navigate the world. But they face a fundamental challenge:

**Physical objects and real-world services are silent to machines.**

When you stand in front of a painting in a museum, you can read the placard. When an AI agent encounters that same painting through a photo, it must *guess* what it's looking at. Sometimes it guesses correctly. Sometimes it confidently provides completely wrong information.

This isn't just about art. It affects:

- **Restaurants:** Agents recommending dishes without knowing current availability or ingredients
- **Shops:** Agents describing products based on outdated or incorrect information
- **Properties:** Agents unable to help visitors follow instructions or access services
- **Documents:** Agents unable to verify authenticity or access metadata
- **Events:** Agents working from cached information that's no longer accurate

The result? AI agents that hallucinate, waste computational resources researching what should be readily available, and frequently fail their users at critical moments.

---

## The Vision

**What if every physical object and service could speak directly to AI agents?**

Imagine:

- A restaurant's menu that tells an agent exactly what's available today, with accurate prices and ingredients
- Museum artwork that provides verified provenance, artist information, and related works
- Legal documents that confirm their authenticity and provide context
- Shop products that give agents accurate specifications straight from the source
- Bus stops that communicate real-time arrival information to agents helping commuters

This is the MX-Card vision: **a standardised way for anything in the physical or digital world to communicate machine-readable context to AI agents.**

---

## The Concept

An **MX-Card** is a machine-readable data container that provides verified information about a subject. Think of it as a "voice" that objects and services can use to communicate with AI agents.

**Key principles:**

1. **Standardised Format:** A consistent structure that any AI agent can understand
2. **Verified Authority:** Mechanisms to confirm that card issuers have legitimate authority over their subjects
3. **Machine-Actionable:** Not just readable by machines, but providing instructions and permissions
4. **Physical-Digital Bridge:** Entry points (like QR codes) that connect physical objects to their digital context
