# MX-Gathering Business Plan (Draft)

## Executive Summary

MX-Gathering monetization combines professional certification with book subscription services, creating recurring revenue whilst maintaining open source community values. The model targets developers, UX designers, and technical architects implementing AI agent interaction patterns.

**Revenue Targets (Year 1):**

- Certification: 500 certifications at £299 average = £149,500
- Subscriptions: 200 individual + 20 team subscriptions = £38,000
- Total projected revenue: £187,500

**Investment Required:** £25,000 (certification platform, examination development, marketing)

## Market Analysis

### Target Market Size

**Primary Markets:**

- **UK:** 500,000+ web developers, 100,000+ UX designers
- **Europe:** 2.5M+ developers, 500,000+ UX professionals
- **Global English-speaking:** 10M+ potential professionals

**Market Timing:**

- January 2026 platform convergence (Amazon Alexa+, Microsoft Copilot Checkout, Google UCP)
- Increased enterprise focus on AI agent compatibility
- Growing demand for validated expertise in emerging technologies

### Competitive Landscape

**Direct Competitors:**

- AWS Certification (machine learning focus, not agent UX)
- Google Cloud Certification (infrastructure, not design patterns)
- No direct competitor for AI agent interaction certification

**Competitive Advantages:**

- First-mover position in agent UX certification
- Author credibility from published MX-Bible and MX-Handbook
- Open source community foundation (trust and visibility)
- Practical patterns validated in production systems

### Market Needs

**Skills Gap:**

- Organisations implementing AI agent features lack validated expertise
- Developers need structured learning paths for agent patterns
- Hiring managers require objective skill assessment

**Knowledge Currency:**

- Rapid evolution of AI agent capabilities requires continuous learning
- Static books become outdated within months
- Professionals need access to latest patterns and case studies

## Risk Analysis

### Market Risks

**Risk:** Slow certification adoption due to unrecognised credential

**Mitigation:**

- Partner with established tech training providers
- Secure endorsements from major platforms (Google, Microsoft, Amazon)
- Publish case studies demonstrating ROI for certified professionals

**Risk:** Fast-evolving technology makes certification obsolete

**Mitigation:**

- Annual examination updates reflecting latest patterns
- Mandatory annual renewal with updated content
- Subscription model ensures continuous learning

### Operational Risks

**Risk:** High development cost for certification platform

**Mitigation:**

- Use existing platforms (Moodle, Canvas) with customisation
- Start with manual proctoring, automate over time
- Phase rollout (Foundation first, advanced tiers later)

**Risk:** Content quality and currency

**Mitigation:**

- Establish advisory board of practitioners
- Regular content review cycles
- Community feedback mechanisms

### Financial Risks

**Risk:** Insufficient initial subscribers to cover fixed costs

**Mitigation:**

- Pre-launch waitlist and early-bird pricing
- Corporate pilot programmes for validation
- Bootstrap approach (minimal fixed costs initially)
